Crossing Iceland 2025

Published July 2025

In the summer of 2025, we set out to cross Iceland from the northern coastline to the southern black sand beaches – completely on foot. This route took us through lava deserts, glacial rivers, mossy valleys, and brutally remote landscapes. We carried everything on our backs and avoided any paid accommodation or resupply points.

Route Overview

Starting at the town of Akureyri in the north, we followed a mix of off-trail navigation, F-roads, and hiking routes through the Highlands. Major points included Askja caldera, Nýidalur, the Sprengisandur desert, and finally descending via Þórsmörk into Skógar.

Challenges

  • Multiple glacial river crossings
  • High winds and snow in the Highlands (even in July!)
  • Carrying 8–10 days of food at a time

Gear Highlights

We used ultralight tents, trail running shoes, and homemade dehydrated meals to save weight. Navigation was done via GPS and printed maps as backup.

Lessons Learned

Iceland rewards those who are prepared, humble, and flexible. We learned a lot about ourselves, the land, and each other.
